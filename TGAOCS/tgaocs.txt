--THE GOLDEN AGE OF COMPUTER SCIENCE--

--INTRODUCTION--

This essay covers a series of events from 1940-2000. It covers each decade individually and its impact on modern computers.


--1940's--

1940 - The Z2 computer is built by Konrad Zuse.
It was a mechanical, relay-based machine that replaced the arithmetic logic from the original Z1 (which Zuse had built in his parent's home) with 600 relay circuits, weighing over 600lbs. (~272kgs.)
[SOURCE: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z2_(computer)]

1941 - Konrad Zuse builds the Z3, a electromagnetical, programmable, machine that pioneered modern computer science features, such as floating-point numbers.
It had ~2000 relays and a 22-bit word length.
The Z3 had a clock frequency of around 5-10hz.
Additionally, ZuseKG was founded in Berlin, with the sole purpose of making computers.
[SOURCE: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer]

1942 - John Mauchly, a physicist attending Moore School, proposes a general-purpose electronic computer.
Lieutenant Herman Goldstine, the liasion between the Army and Moore School, asks Mauchly to make a formal proposal for the first edition of the ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer).
Additionally, the Z4 is built in Berlin, though partially.

[SOURCES: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Mauchly AND https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z4_(computer)]

1943 - In April, the Army contracted Moore School to start building the ENIAC, with John Mauchly leading the conceptual design.
It was confidential, under the name "Project PX".
Because of the ENIAC, John Mauchley, in 2002, was inducted into the National Inventors Hall Of Fame.
The ENIAC was capable of extremely fast calculations, and solving equations which had not been solved before.
It could do arithmetic, square rooting, I/O functions, conditional branches, etc.

[SOURCE: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Mauchly]

1944 - John Mauchley and J. Presper Eckert, a graduate of Moore School, begin plans for a second computer called the EDVAC.
John von Neumann, a contributor to the Manhattan Project, creates the ASCC, the first programmable computer in the USA.
It was also called the Harvard Mark I.

[SOURCES: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Mauchly AND https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_Mark_I]
